## What The Syllabi Tend To Cover

- Social preferences and bargaining.
- Trust, reciprocity, and public goods.
- Coordination and repeated games.
- Risk and time preferences.
- Auctions and market institutions.
- Industrial organization exercises.
- Some behavioral extensions such as reference dependence, self-control, and overconfidence.

## Gaps In The Current Bundle

The current bundle already covers social preferences, bargaining, trust/public goods, auctions, and several market exercises, and the original first-wave behavioral additions from this scan are now in place. The clearest remaining gaps are:

- Self-control, commitment, ambiguity aversion, and loss aversion.
- Voting, public choice, and median-voter style exercises.
- Matching and market design.
- Discrimination and overconfidence.

## First-Wave Additions Now Implemented

## Inference Notes

- The syllabus scan is deliberately conservative: the recommendation favors topics that appear repeatedly across experimental and behavioral economics teaching materials.
- Where a source only implied a topic through a course description, the roadmap treats that as a teaching signal rather than a strict requirement.
